Natural caffeine

For even more flavour!

  • Caffeine, consumed for centuries in many cultures is found naturally in many foodstuffs like tea leaves, coffee beans, and cocoa beans, it can also be manmade.
  • The unique taste of caffeine in Coca-Cola adds to the flavour that's loved by people around the world.
  • The amount of caffeine in Coca-Cola is about 24 mg per 250 ml serving. This is significantly less than the quantity of the same amount of brewed coffee indice and is also less than the amount present ina a 25-30 ml cup of espresso coffee.
  • Modarate caffeine consumption for adults is conidered to be about 300 mg per day.

Phosphoric Acid

To add to the taste

  • Phosphoric acid contains phosphorus, a mineral found in a lot of foodstuffs.
  • Phosphoric acid helps to giv Coca-COla its deliciously tangy taste.
  • A 250 ml serving of Coca-Cola provides 43 mg of phosphorus.
  • The same amount of milk has about 208 mg of phosphorus, one cup of cooked chicken (580 grams) has about 230 mg of phosphorus, and one cup of cooked white rice (150 grams) has about 90 mg of phosphorus.

Caramel E150d

For that dark, distinctive appearance

  • Caramel colours have been used in foods since the 19th century and we've been using it since 1886.
  • Caramel colour is found in a wide range of products, including soft drinks, beer, spirits, bakery products, cereals, sauces, ice-creams, soups, meats and spice blends, etc.
  • Caramel colour E150g is the food additive that gives Coca-Cola its rich dark and distinctive colour.
  • Caramel E150d is one of four categories of caramel that are approved for the use as food colorant in the European Union.
